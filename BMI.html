FIND BMI

Categorie fo BMI

This is for informational purposes only. For medical advice or diagnosis, consult a professional.

Under-weight

If your BMI is underweight, it means your weight is lower than what is considered healthy for your height.

Potential Health Risks Associated with Being Underweight:

  • Malnutrition: Not getting enough nutrients can lead to deficiencies in essential vitamins and minerals, which can weaken your immune system, slow growth and development, and cause various health problems.  
  • Muscle Loss: Underweight individuals may have less muscle mass, which can lead to weakness, fatigue, and decreased bone density.  
  • Hormonal Imbalances: Being underweight can disrupt hormone production, which can affect menstrual cycles in women and other bodily functions.  

Normal

A healthy BMI range for adults is generally considered to be between 18.5 and 24.9.  

  • BMI is just one indicator of health: It's a simple calculation based on height and weight, and it doesn't account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, or body fat distribution.    
  • Other factors to consider: A healthy weight is more than just a number on the scale. It's also about how you feel about your body, your overall health, and your ability to live an active and fulfilling life.    

If you have any concerns about your weight or health, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian. They can help you determine a healthy weight range for you and provide personalized guidance.  

Over Weight

A BMI (Body Mass Index) in the overweight range generally falls between 25 and 29.9.  

Potential Health Risks Associated with Being Overweight:

  • Type 2 Diabetes: Overweight individuals are at higher risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and stroke.  
  • Stroke: Obesity significantly increases the risk of stroke.  
  • Mental Health: Overweight and obesity can sometimes contribute to low self-esteem and other mental health issues.  

Obesity

A BMI (Body Mass Index) of 30 or higher is generally considered to be in the obesity range.  

Obesity is a serious health condition that significantly increases the risk of various health problems, including:

Potential Health Risks Associated with Being Obesity:

  • Type 2 Diabetes: Overweight individuals are at higher risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and stroke.  
  • Stroke: Obesity significantly increases the risk of stroke.  
  • Mental Health: Overweight and obesity can sometimes contribute to low self-esteem and other mental health issues.  
  • Sleep Apnea: Obesity has been linked to an increased risk of several types of cancer, including breast, colon, endometrial, and kidney cancer.  
  • Certain Cancers: A sleep disorder that causes breathing to repeatedly stop and start.
  • Osteoarthritis: Excess weight puts extra strain on joints, increasing the risk of osteoarthritis.  

It's crucial to remember:

  • BMI is just one indicator of health. It's a simple calculation based on height and weight, and it doesn't account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, or body fat distribution.  
  • Lifestyle Changes:: If you are obese, making lifestyle changes such as improving your diet and increasing physical activity can help you lose weight and reduce your risk of health problems.
